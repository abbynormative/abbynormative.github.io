About
Hi! I'm a web developer, CFO of a small hardware startup, activist, and occasional freelance journalist. I'm currently earning a Master's degree in data science at the Rochester Institute of Technology while volunteering with Oakland Privacy Group, where I recently completed a small research project investigating hidden biases in how consumers make use of surveillance technology at home. In previous lives I've been a paralegal, a legal secretary, an attendant for a man with cerebral palsy, and an admin at a medical provider's office, helping clients navigate the sometimes tricky medical insurance process.

I'd like to find part-time work as a coder, researcher, or non-profit general support. I'm most interested in doing web development, data analytics, or software engineering with non-profits. My skills include: JavaScript, Python, R, HTML, CSS, Java, automated testing, and data visualization. I have experience working on both in-person and remote teams as a software engineer, data analyst, and digital producer.
